ANCIENT ROME

This chapter covers the Roman Empire between the time period of 30BC to 400AD

There are two types of Roman citizens:

  • Patricians
  • Plebians

The 'plebians' were the poor people in the Roman Empire

The 'patricians' were the rich people in the Roman Empire

The Roma army was very big. The soilders were very disiplined, they were trained to march more than 30 kilometres per day

The army was divided into legions. Each legion had about 5,000 soilders and there were 30 legions in total

The soilders had to serve for 20 years. They had to Roman sitizens to most were sons of previous soilders

The Roman Empire also had an auxilia. These was a back up army

Patricians lived very well compared to the plebians. A very rich Roman family would live in a big country house called a villa. In the town they're house was called a domus.

The plebians lived in blocks of flats called insulaes. these houses were built out of wood and this is why there were no fires allowed in them cause they can burmn down.

In these times education was very strict. It was really only patrician sons who were able to afford an education. Girls were not allowed go to school they're main job was to stay at home and clean and provide children.

They're main source of entertainment were the Roman baths.
